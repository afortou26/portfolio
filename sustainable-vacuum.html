Sustainable Vacuum Redesign

A handheld vacuum designed from the ground up for circularity — every component mapped to a biological or technical nutrient cycle, using hempwood, recycled plastics, sunflower bioplastic, and aluminium.

Course
IND 271, Syracuse
Role
Solo Designer
Tools
SolidWorks, sketching
Framework
Cradle to Cradle
vacuum-hero.jpg — isometric view of the vacuum

The brief

Redesign a consumer product — in this case, a handheld vacuum — to align with Cradle to Cradle principles. Every material must be assigned to either a biological nutrient cycle (compostable, biodegradable) or a technical nutrient cycle (infinitely recyclable or reusable) — with no landfill as an acceptable end-of-life.

Most consumer electronics fail this test: mixed materials bonded with adhesives, proprietary parts, and housing designed to resist disassembly. The goal was to design a product that's easier to separate than to throw away.

Form development

The form language takes cues from organic, curved handheld tools — referencing the ergonomic grip of a power drill while pushing toward something more sculpted. The nozzle sweeps outward with a bulbous profile, giving the vacuum a distinctive silhouette that reads as considered rather than generic.

The handle splits into two mirrored halves that are fastened with exposed aluminium screws — making disassembly obvious and tool-accessible. There are no hidden clips, no glue, no bonded assemblies that require destruction to separate.

Material system

Each part was selected for a specific end-of-life pathway. The result is a product with no mixed-material conflicts — every component can follow its designated cycle without contaminating another.

Hempwood + Soy Adhesive
Handle shells (×2)
Fully organic, biodegradable, durable, and heat resistant. Fibers pressed and bonded with soy flour adhesive. Biological nutrient — compostable at end of life.
Smile Plastics
Nozzle
Made from post-consumer plastic waste (yogurt containers, bottles). UV and heat resistant, fully reusable. Technical nutrient — can be reformed into new components indefinitely.
Aluminium
Screws (×3)
Standard fastener material with well-established recycling infrastructure. Infinitely meltable and reformable. Technical nutrient.
Sunflower Bioplastic
Intake grill
Fully biodegradable, injection-moldable, coatable with sunflower-derived varnish for water resistance. Biological nutrient — breaks down cleanly after use.

Outcome

The Sustainable Vacuum Redesign demonstrates that circular product design doesn't require sacrificing form. The resulting object has a strong visual identity — the sweeping nozzle, exposed screws, and translucent handle are design choices, not afterthoughts. Sustainability and aesthetics aren't in tension here; they're the same decision.
