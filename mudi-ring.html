MUDI Ring

A smart ring that reads biometric data — heart rate and pulse patterns — and translates them into real-time mood display on a circular screen. Wearable technology meets emotional self-awareness.

The concept

Rings have been worn as symbols of identity, status, and emotion since prehistoric times. MUDI extends that history into the present — a titanium smart ring that reads your heartbeat via a biometric sensor and uses AI on your phone to interpret mood, then displays an expressive emoji-style face on its circular OLED screen.

The design question was: how do you communicate something as subjective as mood through a product worn on your hand? The answer was semiotics — universally understood face iconography combined with a color language that maps to emotional states.

Semiotics & visual language

Color and expression work together: a green smiling face signals positivity; a red frowning face signals stress or frustration; purple with a queasy expression signals discomfort. The system is legible at a glance, on a screen barely larger than a thumbnail.

The ring's titanium body — cut from sheet stock, rolled to shape, then CNC'd — signals durability, luxury, and modernity. This is not just a health gadget; it's a fashion statement that also happens to know how you feel.

Ideation

I used AI image generation to rapidly explore form factors — necklaces, rings, bracelets — before committing to the ring format. The final form features a wide band with a raised circular display housing, creating a signet-ring silhouette that reads as intentional and premium rather than tech-gadgety.

Interaction & UX

The ring pairs to the user's phone via Bluetooth. A companion app — wireframed in Figma — shows mood history charts, biometric trends, and an AI chat assistant called HappyBot that responds when the ring detects negative mood states. Users can also log context notes to build a richer picture of their emotional patterns over time.

Manufacturing

Rings are cut from titanium sheet stock, rolled to shape, and the display housing is cast separately. The biometric reader, Bluetooth module, and OLED screen are added during assembly. The result is a product that justifies a premium price point through material choice and manufacturing precision — not just software.
